Abstract





This paper highlights the groundbreaking contributions of three pioneering women, Ada Lovelace, Josephine Cochrane, and Hedy Lamarr, whose inventions have had a 
profound impact on modern technology.Ada Lovelace is celebrated as the world’s first computer programmer, creating the first algorithm for a computing machine,
laying the foundation for modern programming. Josephine Cochrane invented the dishwasher, revolutionizing household chores and contributing to energy-efficient,
hygienic appliances. Hedy Lamarr, an actress and inventor, co-developed the technology of frequency hopping, laying the foundation for modern wireless communications.
These women’s inventions not only changed their respective lives but also continued to shape the way we live today, demonstrating the enduring influence of their work on 
modern technology and society.

Introduction





Throughout history, remarkable women like Ada Lovelace, Hedy Lamarr and Josephine Cochrane  have made groundbreaking contributions to science and technology.
From programming the first algorithm to inventing the dishwasher and revolutionising communication, their innovations have had lasting impacts on modern life 
and continue to shape industries today.
