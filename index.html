Abstract

 


This paper highlights the groundbreaking contributions of three pioneering women, Ada Lovelace, Josephine Cochrane, and Hedy Lamarr, whose inventions have had a 
profound impact on modern technology.Ada Lovelace is celebrated as the world’s first computer programmer, creating the first algorithm for a computing machine,
laying the foundation for modern programming. Josephine Cochrane invented the dishwasher, revolutionizing household chores and contributing to energy-efficient,
hygienic appliances. Hedy Lamarr, an actress and inventor, co-developed the technology of frequency hopping, laying the foundation for modern wireless communications.
These women’s inventions not only changed their respective lives but also continued to shape the way we live today, demonstrating the enduring influence of their work on 
modern technology and society.

Ada Lovelace
 
Ada Lovelace is a British mathematician born on the 10th of December 1815, also known as August Ada Byron or  Lady Byron.
In the mid-1800s, she wrote an algorithm for a computing machine, which earned her recognition as “the first computer programmer”.
She was a gifted mathematician who became the mentee of Charles Babbage at age 17.Lovelace didn’t have a typical upbringing for an upper-class girl.
She was taught mathematics and science which was commonly frowned upon for women during that period in history.
(Biography.com Editors website,2021).


modern applications
 
Ada wrote the first algorithm explaining how Charles Babbage’s analytical engine could calculate Bernoulli numbers.
She outlined step-by-step operations that included calculations, storing results and data fetching a process now known as execution trace. 
.She described conditional logic and loops, and she also foresaw the future of using codes to generate music 
and patterns and showing an extraordinary vision for computing(Sarah Baldwin,2018).


impact

Ada wrote codes for machines that were not yet in existence making her the pioneer of computer programmes.
She provided the blueprint for programming which can be attributed to her power of imagination in technology.
This has inspired countless people to pursue careers in science and technology(Ashley Cavanagh,2022).
Ada Lovelace has provided the blueprint for programming inspiring countless people to pursue careers in science and technology. 
She is a symbol of the power of imagination in technology. 


Josephine Cochrane
 
Josephine Cochrane was born on 8th March 1841 in the state of Ohio to her civil engineer father John Garis and her great grandfather John Fitch, 
who was the inventor of steamboat innovations.

She was inspired to invent the dishwasher after her servants chipped her fine China repeatedly. 
She took over dishwashing as they were her prized possessions and found it very tedious, so she decided to find a better solution.
As a result, she worked with a local mechanic to design a machine that used water pressure to clean dishes which she patented in 1886.
This came to be known as  Cochrane’s dishwasher(Mit. edu,2019).

Hedy Lamar

Hedy Lamar was an Austrian-American actor who made advanced contributions to technology
by revolutionising frequency hopping which is now the foundation for GPS, Bluetooth and Wi-Fi.

She was born in 1914 in Vienna, Austria with parents who had notably different interests.
Her father’s interest in the mechanics of how things worked fostered her interest in problem-solving and innovation
while her concert pianist mother cultivated her appreciation for creativity and performance. 
These two contributed to the dual passion for acting and inventing. 
She was later in a controlling marriage to Fritz Mandl, an Austrian arms manufacturer and industrialist who by association introduced her to weaponry and wartime strategies.

Hedy Lamarr and George Antheil, a well-known composer of experimental music were able to devise a system based on ‘frequency hopping’.
This was meant to help torpedoes hit their targets when at the time, they were controlled by radio signals which could be jammed by their enemies.

Frequency hopping involves both the transmitter and receiver rapidly switching between multiple frequencies in a synchronised pattern,
making it nearly impossible for enemies to intercept or disrupt the communication.
Antheil applied his knowledge of synchronisation to make sure he coordinated the complex mechanical and rhythmic patterns
(Colleen Cheslak, 2018).
